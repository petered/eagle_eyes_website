Training & Equipment

Hands-on training in Eagle Eyes for drone teams and public safety organizations — built and delivered by the people who use it in the field.

Good software only helps if your team knows how to use it when it matters. These sessions take crews from first launch to confident night operations, with the checklists and documentation to back it up.

Courses run on your schedule and at your location, for crews of eight or more. Below is what we offer, plainly laid out.

Courses

Each course includes one Eagle Eyes license per participant.

Advanced License & Eagle Eyes Operator Day

A complete weekend: earn your Advanced RPAS license and become a confident Eagle Eyes operator. Starts with a focused online session on the Air Law portion of the Advanced exam, then moves into presentations, the exam itself, flight reviews, and a full operator day in the field.

Schedule
  • Online (pre-course): 4-hour session on the Air Law section of the Advanced License
  • Friday 6–9 PM: Advanced License presentation
  • Saturday 8 AM–12 PM: Advanced presentation  ·  12–2 PM: Advanced License exam
  • Saturday 2–4 PM: Flight reviews
  • Saturday 10 PM–midnight (optional): Night search exercise
  • Sunday 9 AM–4 PM: Eagle Eyes Operator Day — 2 hours theory, field time for the rest of the day
Includes
  • One Eagle Eyes license
  • Flight review
  • Checklists and all documentation required to satisfy Transport Canada and EMCR
