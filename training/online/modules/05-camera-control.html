Module 4

Camera Control

⏱️ 45 minutes

Learning Objectives

  • Switch between wide, zoom, and thermal cameras
  • Understand thermal palettes and when to use each
  • Use the laser rangefinder to measure distances
  • Capture 360° panoramas for area documentation

4.1 Camera Types

DJI enterprise drones supported by Eagle Eyes carry multiple cameras on the same gimbal. You can switch between them at any time during flight using the camera selector buttons on the right side of the Fly Screen (labeled W, T, Z, ZT).

CameraButtonFOVWhat It Does
WideW82-84°The main RGB camera with the widest field of view. Best for general search and area coverage. This is where AI detection runs.
ZoomZ3x-56x opticalOptical zoom camera for investigating detections up close. Use when you spot something and need a closer look without descending the drone.
ThermalT~40°Infrared thermal camera that shows heat signatures. Available on M3T, M30T, M4T, and H30T drones only. See Module 3 for thermal detection details.
Zoom + ThermalZTSplit viewSplit-screen mode showing zoom and thermal side by side. Useful for cross-referencing a heat signature with the visual image simultaneously.
💡
AI detection only runs on the Wide (W) camera feed. When you switch to Zoom, Thermal, or split-screen, the AI detector pauses. Switch back to Wide to resume automated detection.

4.2 Thermal Palettes

When viewing the thermal camera feed, you can change the color palette to control how temperatures are displayed. Different palettes make different features easier to see depending on the conditions.

PaletteAppearanceWhen to Use
White HotHotter objects appear brighter/whiter, cooler objects darkerGeneral purpose — the most commonly used palette. Good in most conditions.
Black HotHotter objects appear darker/blacker, cooler objects lighterUseful in bright daytime conditions where a bright screen is hard to read. Some operators find dark targets easier to spot.
RainbowFull color spectrum from blue (cold) through green/yellow to red (hot)Maximum temperature differentiation. Useful when you need to distinguish between multiple temperature zones, not just hot vs. cold.
IronbowDark purple/blue for cold, through red/orange to bright yellow/white for hotFamiliar "heat vision" display. Good for presentations and reports as it intuitively communicates temperature.
💡
Start with White Hot for most SAR operations. Switch to Rainbow if you need to see subtle temperature differences in a complex scene.

4.3 Laser Rangefinder

Enterprise DJI drones equipped with a laser rangefinder can measure the distance from the drone to any point the camera is aimed at. This is useful for estimating how far a detection is from the operator, measuring distances between landmarks, or documenting scene dimensions.

SpecificationValue
Range3m – 1,200m
Accuracy±0.2m at 100m

How to Measure

  1. Point the gimbal at the target you want to measure
  2. Tap the Laser button on the Fly Screen
  3. The measured distance appears on screen
  4. The GPS coordinates of the laser point are calculated and placed on the map as a ⊕ Laser Point marker

Laser points persist on the map for the duration of the session and can be used as reference markers for ground teams.

4.4 360° Panorama New in 1.6.0

Eagle Eyes can capture ultra high-resolution 360° panoramas by automatically rotating the drone and stitching multiple images together. This produces a comprehensive view of the area for documentation, briefings, or post-mission analysis.

Panorama Zoom Modes

ModeDetail LevelCapture TimeBest For
StandardCurrent zoom levelFastestGeneral area documentation
2X ZoomHigher detailModerateBalance of speed and detail
7X ZoomMaximum detailLongestDetailed scene documentation where you need to zoom into areas later

Capturing a Panorama

  1. Fly to desired altitude (60-100m typical for a good overview)
  2. Ensure the drone is in a stable hover
  3. Tap ☰ Menu → Capture → 360° Panorama
  4. Select your zoom mode (Standard, 2X, or 7X)
  5. Tap Start Capture
  6. Wait for the automated sequence to complete — the drone will rotate itself
⚠️
Do not touch the flight controls during panorama capture. The automated gimbal/yaw sequence needs to complete without interruption. If interrupted, the panorama will be incomplete.

Thermal Panoramas

On thermal-equipped drones (M3T, M30T, M4T, H30T), you can capture thermal panoramas by switching to the Thermal camera before starting the capture. This produces a 360° thermal overview that is excellent for identifying heat sources spread across a wide area.

When to use panoramas: Documenting a search area before/after sweeps, creating reference imagery for ground teams, recording incident scenes for reports.

When NOT to use: During active subject search (use live detection instead), time-critical operations, or when subjects may be moving.
