Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Eagle Eyes Pilot and Scan

Getting Started

What is Eagle Eyes Pilot?

Eagle Eyes Pilot is an all-in-one drone app for Search and Rescue that detects color and motion anomalies in real time, and streams images and live drone track directly to the team's CalTopo map.

What hardware/system requirements are needed?

Pilot: Android 8+ devices with 2GB storage, DJI SDK-4/5 compatible. Requires Android 7+ with processor comparable to Snapdragon 855 (Galaxy S10 level), 6GB+ free storage. Note: Incompatible with Google Pixel 6/7.

Scan: Windows 10/11 or MacOS Ventura/Sonoma; modern CPU; 8GB+ RAM recommended; no GPU required currently. For Mac, ARM64 architecture (M1, M2, M3, or M4 chipsets) is strongly recommended.

What drones and controllers is Eagle Eyes Pilot compatible with?

Eagle Eyes Pilot is compatible with many DJI drones and controllers using SDK-4 and SDK-5. This includes Mavic 3 series, M30 series, and many others. Check the official compatibility list for your specific drone model.

Does the software work offline?

Yes, both Scan and Pilot operate completely offline. Internet is required only for initial license key activation on the device.

Is Eagle Eyes available on iOS App Store or Google Play?

Eagle Eyes is available only through the website download, not app stores. iOS is not supported because DJI has discontinued iOS SDK development.

Detection System

How does the detector work?

The system detects objects based on color or motion. It identifies up to three anomalies per frame, scoring 0-1. It targets small color clusters and isolated moving objects when the frame is stationary.

What is the difference between different detector modes?

Normal: Shows highest scoring detections only.
Hyper-sensitive: Shows all detections.
Silent: Background recording without screen display.
Off: Detector inactive.

Why does Eagle Eyes sometimes detect wrong objects or miss people?

The color/motion analysis highlights "blobs" that stand out from their surroundings. Strong color contrast works best; brightly colored leaves can trigger false positives, while shadows and low contrast reduce accuracy.

The motion detector isn't picking up movement?

The motion detector requires a stable background; wind or camera movement reduces effectiveness. Scene complexity can cause motion to be deprioritized when many color anomalies exist.

Does the detector work with thermal footage?

Yes, although it's designed primarily for color/motion, it functions on thermal. It excels at detecting motion on stationary thermal feeds and warmer objects.

How should I fly the drone for best results?

Recommended: 50m high, camera pitched 70° down, moving slowly enough to avoid blur. Consider using the Danish Emergency Management Agency's Overview, Focused, Systematic (OFS) method.

Livestreaming & CalTopo

How does livestreaming work?

Eagle Eyes Pilot enables wireless livestreaming to remote viewers via QR code. It supports up to three simultaneous viewers. Performance is optimized when devices share the same hotspot or Wi-Fi network.

How do I connect to CalTopo?

In Pilot, select Fly → three lines (top right) → "Connect to CalTopo" → sign in → choose map. The Beta version allows changing maps via QR code.

Can I fly offline and upload drone tracks to CalTopo?

Yes, Pilot reads DJI flight logs automatically. You can upload GPS tracks to CalTopo in a few clicks once online. Real-time tracking requires a paid license; offline upload is free.

How do I get clean HDMI output from my drone controller?

DJI Pilot 2: Open menu → HD → change "Duplicate Screen" to "Camera View."
Eagle Eyes Pilot: Menu → Livestreaming → toggle "Clean HDMI Output" on.

Using Multiple Apps

Can I fly with DJI app and run Eagle Eyes Pilot simultaneously?

Yes, you can easily switch between DJI Pilot 2 and Eagle Eyes Pilot during flight. Use the in-app shortcut or back button on the controller to toggle between applications.

How do I use Eagle Eyes Pilot and Scan together?

Transmit live video and coordinates to your laptop via HDMI cable and capture card. Fly either directly in Pilot or use DJI Pilot 2 while Pilot runs in the background for coordinate transmission.

How do I use Eagle Eyes Scan to analyze live drone video?

Connect your drone controller HDMI output to laptop via capture card. Open Scan, select "📺 HDMI Livestream," double-click video source, enable recording, and activate the detector with the eagle button.

Flight Logs & Data

Do flights made with Eagle Eyes Pilot sync to Airdata?

Yes, as of Pilot 1.5.0, flight logs sync to Airdata. Logs are copied to the DJI-Log folder on Pilot startup and normally sync within minutes of reconnecting to internet.

How do I get coordinates for detections in Scan?

For DJI MP4s: Enable "Subtitle" recording in DJI Pilot 2 to create .SRT files. For live HDMI: Ensure laptop and controller are on the same Wi-Fi. For other drones: Import CSV flight records or extract EXIF from images.

How to ensure drone GPS data is recorded with DJI Pilot 2 videos?

Enable video captions (.SRT files) in DJI Pilot 2 settings. Load both .SRT and .MP4 files into Eagle Eyes Scan for proper frame geolocation.

How do I review detections?

Recommended method: Run the detector on recorded footage in Eagle Eyes Scan. Alternative: View past detections directly in Pilot via the three-dot menu or film roll icon.

Troubleshooting

Black screen when connecting to drone?

This occasionally occurs on M30T and M350 models. Try switching to the DJI app and back, or reboot the RC controller. As a fallback, use the standard DJI piloting app if the issue persists.

How do I recalibrate the gimbal in Pilot?

In piloting view, tap the three horizontal lines (top right) → Drone → options include "Recenter Gimbal" and "Calibrate Gimbal."

How do I drop a marker and return to an area after battery swap?

Tap the pin/camera icon under the map → choose "No photo, just add marker" → marker saves at current location. Return by tapping the saved point → "++" circle icon to draw a navigation line.

Licensing & Safety

Is it risky to use Eagle Eyes Pilot Beta?

Using the Eagle Eyes Pilot Beta app does not compromise the drone's core flight systems or safety. It's built on the DJI SDK, and all flight-critical functions remain under DJI firmware control.

Can I run Beta and Stable versions on the same device?

Yes, both versions can operate simultaneously on a single device since licensing applies per device rather than per app version. Beta has a yellow logo; Stable has a blue logo.

Can I share my license with others?

Licenses are machine-locked to one device. Multiple users within an organization can share that device, but additional devices require separate keys.

What is Eagle Eyes' privacy and data security policy?

Your data always stays local, under your control, and is never uploaded to Eagle Eyes servers.

How do I share feedback or report detector errors?

Contact info@EagleEyesSearch.com. You can use the feedback form or footage submission form on the website. Your feedback and contributions are greatly appreciated!

Other Platforms

Does Eagle Eyes work with Autel drones?

Pilot is currently DJI-only. Scan works with Autel for pre-recorded footage and live feeds via HDMI.

Does Eagle Eyes work with Skydio drones?

Pilot is limited to DJI. Scan can process pre-recorded Skydio footage and live HDMI feeds. Note: Live coordinates are not displayed with Skydio.

Can I run Eagle Eyes on live feed from an airplane or helicopter?

Yes, several teams use Scan on manned aircraft. All you need is an HDMI video signal that can be fed into a laptop or Windows tablet onboard the aircraft.
