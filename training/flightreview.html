About the flight review

During the review, a certified RPAS Flight Reviewer will assess your ability to plan, brief, and safely conduct an RPAS flight in accordance with Transport Canada requirements.

The session includes a review of required documents, site assessment, emergency procedures, pre-flight checks, flight operations, and post-flight procedures.

After the review, your result is submitted through Transport Canada's Drone Management Portal. If successful, you can apply for your Pilot Certificate, Advanced Operations through your Transport Canada Drone Management Portal account.

Transport Canada requires pilots to pass both the Advanced RPAS exam and an in-person flight review before applying for the Advanced certificate.

What's included

  • In-person Advanced RPAS flight review
  • Assessment by a certified RPAS Flight Reviewer
  • Review of required documents and aircraft registration
  • Pre-flight planning and site assessment
  • Review of normal and emergency procedures
  • Practical flight assessment
  • Post-flight debrief
  • Submission of your flight review result to Transport Canada

Before booking, you must have

  • Passed the Transport Canada Advanced RPAS exam
  • A valid government-issued ID showing your name and date of birth
  • Your Drone Management Portal account information and Advanced exam result/reference information
  • A registered RPA for the flight review, weighing 250 g up to and including 25 kg. You may bring your own, or request to use a registered Eagle Eyes Search aircraft.
  • The Certificate of Registration for the RPA used during the review, unless you are using an Eagle Eyes Search aircraft
  • The registration number clearly marked on the RPA, unless you are using an Eagle Eyes Search aircraft
  • Required aircraft and operational documentation, including normal and emergency procedures
  • A flight-ready aircraft, controller, charged batteries, and any accessories needed to safely complete the review, unless you are using an Eagle Eyes Search aircraft

If you do not have a suitable RPA available, you may be able to use a registered Eagle Eyes Search aircraft for the flight review. Please let us know in the comments on the payment page if you need to use one.

What to bring

Please bring:

  • Your RPA, controller, and charged batteries, unless you are using an Eagle Eyes Search aircraft
  • Spare propellers and any tools or accessories needed for safe operation, if bringing your own aircraft
  • Certificate of Registration for the RPA, if bringing your own aircraft
  • Proof that you passed the Advanced RPAS exam
  • Government-issued ID
  • Your Drone Management Portal account information and Advanced exam result/reference information
  • Aircraft operating manual or manufacturer documentation
  • Your normal, emergency, lost-link, and flyaway procedures
  • Any checklists you use for site assessment, pre-flight, flight operations, and post-flight procedures

The RPA used for the review must be registered in Transport Canada's Drone Management Portal, and the registration number must be displayed clearly on the aircraft. If you are bringing your own aircraft, we recommend placing the registration number on the main body of the aircraft rather than on a removable part such as a battery.

Important note

Please arrive with all required documentation and, if bringing your own aircraft, a flight-ready RPA. If the aircraft is not registered, the registration number is not marked on the aircraft, or required documentation is missing, the flight review may not be able to proceed.
